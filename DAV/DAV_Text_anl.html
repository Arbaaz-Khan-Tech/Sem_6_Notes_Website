Understanding TF-IDF and Text Mining steps

TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency)

V.V.V. Important: Components

Term Frequency (TF)

Measures how frequently a term appears in a document

Formula: TF(t,d) = f(t,d) / Î£f(k,d)

Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)

Measures how important a term is across all documents

Formula: IDF(t) = log(N/DF(t))

Pros

  • Simple and intuitive
  • Effective for document ranking
  • Handles common words well
  • Works well with large datasets
  • Easy to implement

Cons

  • Ignores word order
  • Doesn't capture semantic meaning
  • May not work well with short texts
  • Can be sensitive to document length
  • May miss contextual relationships

Text Mining Steps

Preprocessing Steps

  1. Text Cleaning
    • Remove special characters
    • Convert to lowercase
    • Remove numbers
  2. Tokenization
    • Split text into words
    • Handle punctuation
  3. Stop Word Removal
    • Remove common words
    • Filter out noise
  4. Stemming/Lemmatization
    • Reduce words to root form
    • Normalize variations

Analysis Steps

  1. Feature Extraction
    • Bag of Words
    • TF-IDF vectors
    • Word embeddings
  2. Pattern Recognition
    • Topic modeling
    • Sentiment analysis
    • Named entity recognition
  3. Data Mining
    • Clustering
    • Classification
    • Association rules
  4. Evaluation
    • Accuracy metrics
    • Performance analysis
    • Result validation
